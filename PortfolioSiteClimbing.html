The Spanish Adventure

Tom Pearson and I spent 3 weeks over New Years roaming 'round Spain in his van. Spending time in Siurana (Catalunya), Chulilla (Valencia), Riglos (near Huesca) and a final stop before our ferry home in drizzly Font! Travelling with the van gives you so much freedom to stop wherever, sleep wherever, climb wherever and provides a little homeliness to your travels.

The Golden Hour

The hour preceding the setting of the sun provides a majestic, radiant light prime for some late sending photo opportunities! However like all good things, this doesn't last forever and soon the headtorches and lamps were out for a spooky night session.

In Too Deep

The coming of the the end of the summer marks the start of the Britich deep water solo season. The months of glorious sunshine beaming down from the skies onto the seas means August, Septemebr and even October give us our warmest sea temperatures! This signals the mass migration of psyched climber folk down to esoteric sea cliffs and zawns around the UK to get a piece of the action! This year we joined in at Pembroke and Berry Head, Devon.

Benign Lives

The cold northern winter temperatures bring the present of friction, perfect for precarious highball slab climbing, this time we tackled the Johnny Dawes classic "Benign Lives" 7a+ at Froggatt, Peak District
